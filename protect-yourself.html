// Page 03 — How to Protect Yourself

Your Defense Starts Here

Staying protected from AI phishing does not require any technical expertise — it just requires some awareness and employing certain habits.

// 01 — Detection

How to Spot an AI Phishing Attack

If in doubt, always use an alternative means of communication to verify the legitimacy of a message. Do not use the contact information provided within the suspicious message itself. For instance, if the message includes a phone number, do not call the provided number — look up the organization's number independently instead.

Recognizing the behavioral aspects of AI phishing is likely to be far more effective than looking for technological aspects. Focus on what the message is asking you to do — not how it looks.

// 02 — Incident Response

What to Do If You've Been Targeted

If you believe you have fallen for a phishing attack, act quickly:

  1. Change your passwords immediately, starting with your email account, from a separate trusted device.
  2. Alert your bank if you believe your financial information may have been compromised.
  3. Run a security scan on your devices to ensure you do not have any malware.
  4. If your personal information was stolen, visit IdentityTheft.gov to learn the steps you can take.
  5. Report the attack to the FTC at ReportFraud.ftc.gov or forward phishing emails to reportphishing@apwg.org.

// 03 — Prevention

Good Habits to Stay Protected

Staying protected from AI phishing does not require any technical expertise — it just requires some awareness and employing certain habits:

01 //

Don't Click Unexpected Links

Do not click links included in emails or texts that you did not expect to receive. Instead, go to the company's website directly by typing the address yourself.

02 //

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication

Enable multi-factor authentication on all of your important accounts in case your password may get stolen. This adds a critical second layer of protection.

03 //

Keep Software Up to Date

Make sure that your devices and software are up to date. Updates frequently include critical security patches that protect against the latest known threats.

04 //

Stay Skeptical

Be skeptical of any message that creates a sense of urgency, asks for personal information, or seems too good to be true. When in doubt, verify through another channel.

Every internet user should learn how to recognize and report AI-powered phishing attempts.
